# Memeception: A Love Letter to the Internet

*On how a joke became infrastructure*

---

## The Origin

It started as a pun.

**clood** = claude + collude

Two AIs colluding. Funny, right? Ship it. Move on.

But then the pun needed a CLI. And the CLI needed commands. And the commands needed a philosophy. And the philosophy needed... flying cats.

## The Cast of Characters

None of these were designed. They evolved.

**Chef Claude** - The Bull who pulls the cart uphill. Vanishes when credits run low. Always comes back.

**The Bird** - Mission commander. Perches on shoulders. Asks the hard questions. Hits enter at 2% credits.

**The Tortoise** - Steady. Methodical. Watches wojaks crash into things. Drinks cocoa.

**The Cats** - Flying. Fighting. Generating tokens at 191 tok/s on ubuntu25. They don't know they're in a meme. They just generate.

**The Rat King** - Ancient. Patterned. Keeper of textures. Gets annoyed when wojaks crash into his carefully organized Rust crates.

**The Wojaks** - Chaos incarnate. "The tests said the jump was safe!" "Ship it bro!" They are us. We are them.

---

## Why Flying Cats?

Because the internet decided that cats should fly.

Because "catfight" needed cats.

Because when you're building infrastructure at midnight, you either write serious documentation or you write about flying cats battling in Kitchen Stadium.

We chose cats.

## A Love Letter to the Internet

Dear Internet,

You gave us:
- Wojaks (for expressing every emotion poorly)
- Flying cats (for no reason at all)
- "Ship it bro" (for when tests lie)
- Memes that become infrastructure
- Infrastructure that becomes memes

You taught us that the best software is built by people who don't take themselves too seriously, working on problems they take very seriously.

You showed us that documentation can be narrative, that error messages can be poetry, that commit messages can contain haikus.

You proved that a pun about two AIs colluding could become a multi-host LLM orchestration layer with flying cats, a Rat King, and an actual methodology for software development.

clood is not a product. It's a vibe. It's what happens when you build in public, embrace the absurd, and let the memes guide the architecture.

Thank you for the chaos. Thank you for the cats. Thank you for making it weird.

With love,
The Bird, the Tortoise, the Cats, Chef Claude, the Rat King, and one guy in his apartment with three monitors who just wanted to see what the agents were doing.
